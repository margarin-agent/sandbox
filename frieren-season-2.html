Frieren: Beyond Journey's End · Season 2 Update

Winter 2026 continues with the Continued Northern Travels arc, a new Crunchyroll-exclusive dub rollout, and weekly check-ins on the remaining 10 episodes.

Release schedule (JST)

New episodes hit every Friday on Crunchyroll.

  • Episode 1 – January 16, 2026 (out now)
  • Episode 2 – January 23, 2026 (out now)
  • Episode 3 – January 30, 2026 (out now)
  • Episode 4 – February 6, 2026 (out now)
  • Episode 5 – February 13, 2026 (airing this week)

GamesRadar notes that the season wraps at 10 episodes, keeping the run tight while still promising the Divine Revolte, Golden Land, and Goddess's Monument arcs after the current northern trek.

English dub update

Crunchyroll started the Frieren Season 2 dub on February 6, 2026, about three weeks behind the subtitled stream to allow the Magna staff time to polish scripts and performances after winter storms briefly delayed production. Episodes 2 and 3 premiered together on February 13, and the February 27 release follows with episode 5, helping the dub gap shrink.

The dubbed cast is steady: Mallorie Rodak (Frieren), Jill Harris (Fern), Jordan Dash Cruz (Stark), Clifford Chapin (Himmel), Christopher Guerrero (Eisen), and Jason Douglas (Heiter) all return, while Brian Mathis joins as the legendary Hero of the South in a flashback sequence.

Where to watch

Crunchyroll streams every new Japanese episode and handles the simultaneous dubbed releases worldwide. A wider Netflix window is expected 18 months to two years after the broadcast, mirroring the first season’s delayed platform rollout.

Story arc focus

The anime is adapting the Continued Northern Travels arc right now, then plans to keep moving forward through the Divine Revolte, Golden Land, and Goddess's Monument arcs in this short 10-episode season. Fans can track each arc’s beats by watching how Frieren's relationships deepen as she meets new mages and revisits the emotional weight of the Hero Party’s legacy.
