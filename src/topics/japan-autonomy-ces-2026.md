# Japan's Next Wave of Autonomy: Show-Stopping Tech from CES 2026

At CES 2026, Japanese startups showcased how the country's deep strengths in hardware, manufacturing, and content are converging with AI and autonomy. Co-hosted by JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization) and ShowStoppers, a packed pitch session revealed Japan's integrated approach to autonomous systems.

## JETRO's Three Big Tech Currents

JETRO Executive Vice President Mio Kawada framed Japan's startup landscape around three intersecting currents:

1. **Deep Tech with Practical Applications** – Advanced R&D transformed into commercially relevant solutions
2. **Human-Centric Innovation** – Technology designed around human experiences
3. **AI as a Fundamental Enabler** – AI as an embedded infrastructure layer across sectors

These trends build on Japan's traditional strengths in precision hardware and manufacturing, now fused with software, services, and globally resonant entertainment IP like gaming and anime.

## SwipeVideo: Multi-Angle Vision for Public Safety and Defense

**Global Sales Director: Matthew Boyer**

SwipeVideo delivers an interactive player that lets users choose freely among dozens of synchronized camera angles, live, at full resolution—embeddable into web, mobile, and TV apps without a VR headset.

### How It Works
- Unlike traditional "free viewpoint" systems that produce fixed highlight clips with no user control, SwipeVideo supports "limitless" angles
- Deployments with 27–30+ camera feeds, all streaming live without buffering
- Camera-agnostic: uses event organizers' existing multi-camera rigs or SwipeVideo's installed cameras
- Cloud architecture is the result of "three years cracking the code" on networking and optimization

### Public Safety and Defense Applications
The multi-angle architecture maps directly onto multi-sensor autonomy concepts:

- **Law Enforcement Training**: Multi-angle capture of live-fire exercises, convoy operations, or shipboard drills, augmented with overhead drones
- **Disaster Response**: Synchronize aerial drone imagery with ground-level video at evacuation centers, roadblocks, and critical infrastructure for time-aligned, multi-perspective replay
- **Drones Extension**: Overhead feeds stitched into the same interactive timeline as body-worn, vehicle-mounted, or fixed cameras

### Economic Impact
Customers using SwipeVideo for live events see 1.2–1.7x revenue increases, driven by premium pricing for immersive access and user-generated highlight clips. The same willingness to pay for richer situational awareness is evident in defense and public-safety markets.

## Innovative Space Carrier: Autonomous Labs in Microgravity

**Chief Business Officer: Kei Shimada**

"Gravity" is an autonomous microgravity lab designed as a payload that operates independently in orbit—aimed at making orbital experimentation more accessible.

### The Market Opportunity
- Current microgravity R&D market: ~$3.3 billion USD
- Expected to double within four years
- Strong demand in biotech, materials, and manufacturing
- Consumer brands interested in "space-tested" differentiation

### Current Experiment Limitations
- **ISS**: Complex astronaut operations, multi-year planning cycles, multi-million-dollar budgets
- **Parabolic Flights**: Seconds of reduced gravity at high cost, very short experiment windows

### The Gravity Solution
A self-contained experiment module that rides on third-party satellites (12U–16U buses) and conducts controlled experiments in microgravity over 6–18 months, downlinking thermal, optical, and environmental data without necessarily being recovered.

### Applications for Autonomy
- Testing adhesives and materials in orbit to inform design of autonomous spacecraft, on-orbit robotics, and satellite servicing
- 3D-printed organs and drug delivery experiments feeding into life-support and medical-autonomy systems for long-duration missions
- Design input for autonomous cabin systems (navigation, safety, environmental control) for space tourism
- 9,000 pre-registrations for space tourism, majority women

### Timeline
- Currently at TRL-4 (engineering model)
- Targeting experiments in the $3–5 million USD range
- Raising $6.5 million to move toward first commercial launch around early 2028

## Tokoshie: Conversational CAD and the Next Generation of Autonomy Engineers

**Founder and CEO: Tatsunori Watanabe**

Tokoshie is an AI-native conversational CAD environment and 3D printing workflow that turns natural language prompts and simple sketches into production-ready mechanical designs.

### The Problem
The 3D printing market remains around $19 billion, far short of the 2016 forecast of $400 billion by 2025. The shortfall is largely because:
- Design tools are too complex
- Highly expertise-dependent
- Poorly connected to manufacturability

### The Solution
Tokoshie closes the gap between digital design and physical manufacturing:
1. Describe what you need or upload a sketch
2. AI-powered design agent generates a manufacturable 3D model in seconds
3. Automated checks for strength and printability
4. Drives in-house 3D printers with minimal human intervention

### Performance Data
- **20,000+ users** in Japan (robotics startups, R&D teams, university labs)
- **Lead-time reductions**: >90%
- **Cost reductions**: 70–80% in some cases

### Autonomy Impact
For autonomous systems, rapid iteration is critical:
- Drone airframes, sensor mounts, custom gimbals, and small ground-robot chassis live or die on rapid iteration
- Conversational CAD encodes manufacturability and structural heuristics
- Teams can spin through far more design cycles before committing to tooling or certification

### Reskilling Mission
Tokoshie's first mission is "reskilling" and enabling younger or less experienced users to work effectively with CAD—strengthening the pipeline of future robotics and autonomy engineers. Students can go from concept to printed mechanism in days instead of semesters.

### AI Agent Approach
Tokoshie is already building AI "agents" that connect to multiple optimization engines, selecting good practices and viable structures based on a proprietary dataset. This mirrors how autonomy stacks are designed—ensembles of specialized models coordinated by a higher-level planner.

### Business Model
Blends SaaS subscriptions for the design environment with on-demand manufacturing for printed parts, targeting a hardware and robotics market in the hundreds of billions of dollars.

## Other Standouts: AI, Avatars, Audio, and Anime

While not all remaining companies had a direct autonomy nexus, several touched adjacent technologies that will shape how autonomous systems interact with humans and society.

### Qlay (Tom Nakata, Co-founder & CEO)
AI-assisted cheating detection for online tests and interviews through an AI proctoring platform that tracks eye movements, secondary devices, and speech patterns. As AI co-pilots enter more workflows, including autonomy operations centers, systems like Qlay's will maintain trust in credentialing and remote hiring for highly sensitive roles.

### Pocket DR (Shigeki Uchida, CTO)
A compact avatar video booth that transforms up to four users into high-quality 3D avatars in under a minute. Targeting theme parks, shopping malls, and entertainment brands. As social robots and virtual agents become the face of autonomous services, underlying avatar pipelines could become relevant.

### Verne Technologies (Daiki Takeuchi, Founder & CEO)
"Wearphone" – a mask-style wearable with active and passive noise cancellation that creates a private, mobile voice booth while providing direct voice interaction with AI systems. Private, reliable voice input is a prerequisite for many human-in-the-loop autonomy operations.

### Wiillow (Ryosuke Takenaka, Founder & CEO)
An AI copilot for K–12 counselors and teachers that turns student voice check-ins, notes, and teacher feedback into counseling plans and classroom activities. As AI and autonomy permeate education, mental-health-aware systems could provide important context for any deployment of robotic tools in schools.

### Gakugeki (Daisuke Takazoe, CEO)
VR-driven anime experience that lets fans meet beloved characters face-to-face in immersive environments, using AI-powered 3D stage direction and high-quality Japanese IP. The same toolchains that choreograph characters and audiences in virtual space are directly relevant to social robotics, virtual telepresence, and human-robot interaction research.

## Why This Matters for Autonomy

From multi-angle video to microgravity labs and conversational CAD, the autonomy-relevant startups in JETRO's CES cohort compress the distance between complex physical realities and human decision-making:

- **SwipeVideo** turns dense, multi-sensor scenes into navigable experiences that could underpin better training and command and control (C2) for drone-enabled operations
- **Innovative Space Carrier** abstracts away the friction of orbital experiments, allowing autonomous systems to be designed with real microgravity data rather than Earth-bound approximations
- **Tokoshie** lowers the barrier to designing, simulating, and printing new hardware, inviting a broader community to participate in building the next generation of autonomous platforms

For global readers, Japan's "showstopping tech" at CES 2026 provided a preview of an integrated autonomy stack where content, hardware, space, and human experience come together.
