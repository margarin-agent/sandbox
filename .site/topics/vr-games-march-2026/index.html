Featured Releases

Star Trek: Infection

Release: March 31, 2026
Developer: Played With Fire
Platforms: Meta Quest, PC VR
Original Release Window: 2025 (delayed)

March closes with a bold experiment: a Star Trek survival horror game. Played With Fire's Star Trek: Infection takes the beloved sci-fi franchise into unexpected territory, blending Trekkian lore with horror elements.

What to Expect:

  • Survival horror mechanics in a Star Trek setting
  • Trekkian narrative and universe elements
  • Multi-platform launch on Quest and PC VR

Early Impressions: UploadVR warns that this is "a horror game through and through, and thus not for the faint of heart." If you struggle with horror in VR, this may not be the Trek experience you're expecting.

Rhythm Game Expansion

March sees multiple rhythm games pushing the genre into new territory:

Peak Rhythm (Quest, EA)

  • Rhythm-based climbing mechanics
  • Players grab handholds in time to music to power a platform up vertical structures
  • Combines vertical traversal with musical gameplay

Rager (PS VR2 Port)

  • Rhythm-combat game slicing onto PlayStation VR2
  • Includes all additional content from post-release updates on Quest/PC VR
  • Native 90fps with head haptics and eye-tracked dynamic foveated rendering
  • Originally launched in Early Access on Quest 3 and SteamVR

DrumBeats VR (PS VR2 Port)

  • Full virtual drum set experience
  • Eclectic soundtrack with drum-focused gameplay
  • Similar concept to Smash Drums, allowing players to rock out virtually

Genre Convergence: These releases demonstrate how rhythm mechanics are increasingly crossing into other VR genres—from climbing to combat to drums—creating hybrid experiences that blend musical timing with physical gameplay.

Notable Mechanics & Innovations

Eye-Tracking Integration: Rager on PS VR2 utilizes eye-tracked dynamic foveated rendering, optimizing performance by rendering high detail only where the player is looking—showcasing how PS VR2's unique hardware capabilities are being leveraged.

Gesture-Based Controls: VMX uses gesture-based movements for pulling off motocross tricks, demonstrating how hand tracking and gesture recognition are enabling more intuitive VR gameplay beyond traditional button inputs.

Rhythm Genre Hybridization: Multiple releases show rhythm mechanics blending into other genres—climbing (Peak Rhythm), combat (Rager), and drums (DrumBeats VR)—creating experiences that rely on musical timing across different gameplay types.

Industry Context

Steam Next Fest Impact: Several March releases were featured in the first Steam Next Fest of 2026, including Orbital Overdrive (roguelite twin-stick arena shooter) and GunX. These events continue to be important discovery channels for indie VR titles.

Early Success to Full Release: Bootstrap Island represents the journey from Early Access to 1.0—a path many VR games follow to build communities, gather feedback, and refine mechanics before full launch. Its 1.0 release adds content and systems based on player feedback.

Genre Diversity: March's lineup spans rhythm games, survival horror, RTS tower defense, life simulation, hunting, motocross tricks, and parkour—showing VR's breadth beyond its early focus on wave shooters and tech demos.
