NVIDIA GTC 2026: The Next Phase of AI

Overview

NVIDIA's GPU Technology Conference (GTC) returns to San Jose from March 16-19, 2026, with CEO Jensen Huang's keynote kicking off at the SAP Center on Monday. This year's conference promises to be the most significant yet, as NVIDIA—now the world's most valuable company—lays out its vision for AI's next phase. With 30,000 attendees from 190 countries across 10 venues, GTC 2026 focuses on the full AI stack: from chips and infrastructure to models and applications.

NemoClaw: Open-Source AI Agent Platform

Expected Announcement: GTC 2026

NVIDIA is rumored to unveil NemoClaw, an open-source AI agent platform designed specifically for enterprises. This move represents a strategic shift: rather than competing directly with frontier AI labs like OpenAI and Anthropic, NVIDIA is building an ecosystem that keeps developers locked into its hardware and software infrastructure.

The platform will enable companies to deploy long-running, always-on AI agents that can operate continuously across workflows. These agents—called "claws"—can manage calendars, analyze data, handle customer interactions, and execute complex multi-step tasks autonomously.

Strategic Goals:

  • Keep developers building inside NVIDIA's software ecosystem
  • Drive demand for more GPU hardware
  • Provide enterprise-grade alternatives to closed-source agent platforms
  • Leverage NVIDIA's $26 billion investment in open-source models

AI as a "5 Layer Cake"

In a blog post released ahead of GTC, Jensen Huang outlined AI as a five-layer architecture that must scale together:

  1. Energy — The power infrastructure supporting AI compute
  2. Chips — GPU and specialized AI hardware
  3. Infrastructure — Data centers, networking, and cloud platforms
  4. Models — Foundation models and specialized AI systems
  5. Applications — End-user software and services

NVIDIA's strategic advantage lies in its position across multiple layers—particularly in chips and infrastructure—where it can orchestrate coordination between the layers and capture value throughout the stack.

Build-a-Claw Event at GTC Park

Dates: March 16-19, 2026 Location: GTC Park, Cesar Chavez Park, San Jose

GTC attendees can participate in a hands-on Build-a-Claw experience to create their own proactive AI assistants using OpenClaw, described as "the fastest-growing open source project in history."

What Participants Will Do:

  • Name their agent and define its personality
  • Grant access to tools and workflows
  • Deploy using cloud compute or local NVIDIA hardware (DGX Spark, GeForce laptop, Jetson modules)
  • Apply agents to any task: calendar management, vacation planning, workout routines, app development

NVIDIA experts will be onsite to help participants customize and deploy agents. The experience demonstrates NVIDIA's vision for local-first AI—agents that work directly with files, apps, and workflows without relying on cloud services.

Why This Matters

For Developers: NemoClaw and OpenClaw represent NVIDIA's bid to own the AI agent infrastructure layer. If successful, NVIDIA will control the tools developers use to build, deploy, and scale AI agents—locking them into NVIDIA hardware and software.

For Enterprises: Open-source AI agents at enterprise scale could reduce dependence on closed-source platforms like OpenAI's ChatGPT Enterprise or Microsoft's Copilot. NVIDIA's approach offers transparency, customization, and local-first deployment options.

For the AI Industry: NVIDIA's $26B open-source investment signals a fundamental shift in strategy. Rather than building its own closed frontier model, NVIDIA is betting on an open ecosystem where it monetizes the infrastructure layer. This challenges the current model competition between OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and others.

For Hardware: If AI agents become the primary interface for enterprise software, the compute demand will be enormous—always-on agents processing tasks continuously. NVIDIA's hardware investments position it to capture this next wave of AI infrastructure needs.

Key Insight: NVIDIA is executing a classic platform play: provide the infrastructure and tools, let others build the applications, and capture value at the layer where you have structural advantage. The "5 layer cake" framing isn't just philosophy—it's a map to where NVIDIA intends to dominate.
