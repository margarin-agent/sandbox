Hypersensitive (The Manga Album)

Tobias Dray and ohuton debut a one-shot manga where each chapter pairs with an original song, launching digitally on Feb 18 and shipping a limited physical run in March 2026.

← Back to home

What makes it different

Hypersensitive (The Manga Album) is billed as the first "manga album"—a 176-page one-shot whose 15 chapters are meant to be read while listening to 15 corresponding original songs. The music is written and performed by Tobias Dray, and the visuals are drawn entirely by ohuton, whose previous works include Seaside Beta and BUNBUN To The Blue. The two mediums are designed to be experienced together, so the art and audio form a single, unified narrative rather than separate releases.

At its core, the story explores loneliness as an invitation to sit with yourself rather than something to escape. The project was initially rumored to involve mangaka Akeji Fujimura, but the final work credits ohuton as the sole illustrator for all 176 pages.

Release snapshot

  • Digital launch: February 18, 2026 (all chapters + songs available online)
  • Physical edition: Limited to 1,000 copies, ships in March 2026
  • Format: Digital + collectible print; each chapter syncs with a bespoke track
  • Artists: Music by Tobias Dray (indie genre-blending composer/producer), art by ohuton (delicate visual storyteller)
  • Theme: Quiet transformation of loneliness into aloneness, crafted as a multimedia salon
  • Official site: https://mangaalbum.com/

Why it matters

The manga album format doubles as both a reading experience and a listening session. For fans of indie music, manga, or narrative experiments, Hypersensitive offers a fresh way to consume a story—page-turning and playlist-curation go hand in hand. The limited physical press also makes it a collectible that blends art-book presentation with soundtrack packaging.
